\begin{abstract}
I bridge geometric gravity and quantum phases by adding a compact circular angle $\theta$ so kinematics live on $Q=\mathbb{R}^{3,1}\times S^1$. Its gauge connection $A_\theta$ makes a loop-induced transport phase $\hol \equiv \tfrac{q_\theta}{\hbar}\!\oint A_\theta\,d\theta$ observable, strictly $\bmod\,2\pi$—and, crucially, readable at $E=B=0$ along both arms. Three laboratory signatures follow: (i) a $\theta$–Aharonov–Bohm phase with $\Delta\phi=\hol\ \bmodtwopi$ at $E=B=0$ along both arms, (ii) a cross–Hall drift $\Delta y\propto(\partial_yA_\theta)\,\dot\theta\,T^2$ with required sign flips, and (iii) a rotor spectrum $E_\ell=\frac{\hbar^2}{2I}\bigl(\ell-\hol/2\pi\bigr)^2$ that determines an effective inertia $I$. A St\"uckelberg completion yields one mediator mass $m_\theta$ and a shared Yukawa range $\lambda_\theta=1/m_\theta$ across sectors; choosing $Q_\theta$ composition-independent (e.g., $\propto m$ or $B\! -\! L$) satisfies leading EP tests. We state falsification gates for each channel and provide data/code.
\end{abstract}
